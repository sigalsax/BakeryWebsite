Balsamic Glazed Salmon Salad

What You Will Need:

  • 2 filets of fish
  • 2 T. of balsamic vinegar
  • 2 T. of honey
  • A pinch of salt/pepper

Recipe:

  1. Mix the balsamic and honey to create a glaze.
  2. Add a little water as needed to thin out the glaze.
  3. Pat dry the salmon filets and season with salt/pepper
  4. Add the salmon to the pan for one minute on each side and then pour glaze over and cook until salmon is cooked through, about 10 more minutes.
  5. Serve on a bed of salad for a delicious warm summer salad!

Challah

Challah to me is one of my favorite things to make. Besides making a beautiful loaf of bread, I feel a direct connection to my ancestry and family history. Challah, or the braided bread, has a significant role in Jewish history. First cited in the Bible (Numbers 15:18-21), it is used in reference to the Holy Temple in Jerusalem and the offerings given there. The intertwined braid symbolize love, truth, peace, creation, freedom, harmony, and family connection. Ideally the braid should have 12 humps to symbolize the original 12 tribes of Israel.

I use various different recipes for Challah, but my favorite one so far belongs to my long time friend, Annie.

What You Will Need:

  • 4 cups of warm water
  • 3 T yeast
  • 5 lb bag of flour
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 2 T salt
  • 1 cup of coconut oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 T of honey

Recipe:

  1. Combine yeast and warm water. Let sit for 10 minutes to allow yeast to activate
  2. In a mixer, combine flour, sugar, coconut oil.
  3. Add yeast mixure and then add the eggs, salt, and honey
  4. Let dough sit covered for 2 hours (preferrably in a warm place)
  5. Braid Challah and let it raise for 30 minutes
  6. Let it bake for 30-40 minutes on 350F

Oreo Pop

Made for a festive location at my campus synagogue, these Oreo Pops look complicated and intimidating, but are honestly one of the most simple dessert ideas ever. Whenever you're in a cinch you can whip this up by combining Oreos or your favorite cookie and some melted chocolate. Sprinkle with some fancy sprinkles and you have a top notch dessert

What You Will Need:

  • A pack of Oreos or your favorite type of cookie with a filling (this is important to stick the popsicle sticks into)
  • Chocolate (white or regular)
  • A double boiler (or makeshift double boiler)
  • Confectionary lollipop sticks
  • Baking sheet lined with parchment paper

Recipe:

  1. Melt chocolate in double boiler and remove from heat.
  2. Open your pack of Oreos, and one at a time, take a popsicle stick and stuff it into the filing part of your Oreo. This may result in a few broken Oreos until you get it down.
  3. Once the stick is inside the Oreo, dip the Oreo in the melted chocolate until coated the way you would like.
  4. Quickly sprinkle the Oreo with sprinkles and place on parchment lined baking sheet. You can present these Dipped Oreos on a nice platter for a pareve or dairy dessert buffet

Teriyaki Tofu & Roasted Veggie Salad

Everytime I make this I feel like I am enjoying a taste of home. My mom would make this for us multiple times a week as she raised us to be health conscious and well fed. Most people enjoy different, (less healthy hair comforting dishes, but this is definitely a more modern family staple of mine.

The best part is, it doesn't taste like tofu at all! I know, hard to believe but it's that good. Especially when you let the teriyaki sauce do its work and crisp the outside you have a delicious, tangy, and satisfying dish and a true crowd pleaser.

What You Will Need:

  • One package of extra firm tofu (I usually opt for Nasoya Tofu, Extra Firm, 14 Oz)
  • 3 T. of premade teriyaki sauce
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder

Recipe:

  1. Cube tofu into small squares
  2. Place in pan and let grill for 2-3 minutes
  3. Add teriyaki sauce, onion powder, and garlic powder. Let crisp up, turning occasionally for 20 minutes or until desired crispness is achieved.
  4. Serve with pasta and cheese for a full and delicious meal

Banana Chocolate Chip Cake

Recipe:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 F and grease a 13x9" baking dish
  2. In separate bowl of an electric mixer, place mashed banana
  3. Add the melted margarine, sugar, eggs, mayo, soy milk, baking soda, flour, salt and vanilla and beat until combined
  4. Set aside a bowl to prepare topping
  5. Combine the flour, sugar and cinnamon into bowl
  6. Add in the melted margarine and vanilla and mix
  7. Mix by hand until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs
  8. Stir in the chocolate and any shavings that came off the chocolate when you were initially chopping
  9. Pour half the cake batter into pan
  10. Sprinkle the cake with 1/2 of the streusel
  11. Add layer ontop of streusel and on top of the new layer, sprinkle the rest of the streusel
  12. Bake for 1 hour

What you will need (for Cake):

  • 4 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) margarine, melted
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup lowfat mayo
  • 1/2 cup soy milk
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3 cups all purpose flour
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla

What you will need (for Streusel Topping):

  • 1 1/4 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup margarine, melted
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla
  • 7 oz good quality bittersweet chocolate, roughly chopped with a knife
